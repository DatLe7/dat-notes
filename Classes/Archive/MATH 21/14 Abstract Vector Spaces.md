### Simple Properties
1. $\vec{0}$ is unique
2. $0\cdot v=\vec{0}$ for all $v\in V$
3. $a\cdot\vec{0}=\vec{0}$ for all $a\in\mathbb{R}$
4. if $a\cdot v=\vec{0}$ then either a=0 or $\vec{v}=\vec{0}$

### Linear Subspace
**Definition:** Let ($V,+,\cdot$) be a linear space. Let $u\in V$ be a non-empty subset. We say u is a linear subspace, if ($u,+,\cdot$) is a linear space itself. i.e., u is closed under + and $\cdot$
- Rem. the zero vector must be included in the span as well
